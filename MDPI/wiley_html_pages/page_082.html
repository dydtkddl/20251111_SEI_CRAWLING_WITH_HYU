    Lithium Metal Anode: Processing and Interface Engineering

    Batteries & Supercaps
    First published: 4 November 2019Last updated: 1 April 2021

    Description

    Lithium metal anodes offer an extremely high theoretical specific capacity and advantages in terms of volumetric energy density for advanced lithium-ion and all-solid-state batteries. System integration of Li metal anodes is essential for the range extension and advancement of electric vehicles. However, challenges remain, concerning safety and capacity fading due to Li dendrite formation and electrolyte instability. For the development of next-generation batteries beyond established Li-ion anodes, advanced strategies for processing and protection of Li-metal anodes with enhanced safety and durability are necessary.

    Interface engineering, coatings, and electrolyte systems are important areas for research and development for the production and implementation of Li-metal anodes. Together with our guest editors Stefan Kaskel, Qiang Zhang, and Xueliang (Andy) Sun (see picture), we invite you to submit your work on this hot topic for inclusion in this special collection.

    Lithium–Sulfur Batteries

    Batteries & Supercaps
    First published: 2 June 2021Last updated: 16 December 2022

    Description

    Lithium–sulfur batteries offer extremely high specific energies exceeding 400 Wh/kg and are an attractive new technology for applications in large commercial vehicles (e.g., trucks and busses) and aviation, in particular high-altitude long endurance (HALE), high-altitude pseudo satellites (HAPS), and electric vertical take-off and landing (eVTOL). A key challenge is to further improve cycle life, power density, and volumetric energy density of such battery systems for a wider range of applications. New solutions are required to suppress the polysulfide shuttle, to promote the sulfur redox reactions, and to reduce the lithium anode corrosion. The development of novel electrolytes and concepts for safe operation of lithium metal anodes are key drivers in this field. For industrialization, scalable syntheses and processing methods for electrode production are required. Understanding interfacial process and reaction mechanisms profits from advanced analytical tools for the speciation of intermediates and in situ characterization of electrochemical processes. Coating technologies are also essential to enhance the performance of electrodes, separators, and current collectors towards applications.

    Stefan Kaskel

    Jia-Qi Huang

    Hikari Sakaebe

    Batteries & Supercaps, together with Guest Editors Stefan Kaskel (Technische Universität Dresden, Germany), Jia-Qi Huang (Beijing Institute of Technology, China), and Hikari Sakaebe (AIST, Osaka, Japan) invite you to submit your work on this hot topic for inclusion in our upcoming Special Collection!
